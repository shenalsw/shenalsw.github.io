Ceylon Tea

About Ceylon Tea

Introduced in 1867, Ceylon Tea has grown to be the top agriculture export in Sri Lanka and provides direct and indirect employment to nearly 1 million people while around 4% of the country’s land area is covered in tea plantations amounting to nearly 203000 hectares. Sri Lanka is an island made for tea. The country produces tea throughout the year and the total tea production is about 340 million kilograms per annum. Ceylon Tea industry maintains the highest quality in the global tea market and ISO 3720 is the minimum standard applies for the products. The country has the capability to produce the cleanest tea in the world in terms of minimum pesticide residues. Ceylon tea also has a distinct flavor not found in other tea brands, that flavor is governed by the tea being exclusively handpicked giving it the fresh and fine quality.

Types of Tea

Ceylon Black Tea

Ceylon Black Tea is handpicked, traditionally manufactured in the artisanal style, and sourced from a single origin to retain authenticity.
The selection of Black Tea is unblended to ensure the finest quality and packed directly at source within days of harvesting to guarantee freshness in every pack of Ceylon Black Tea. Sip a cup Black Tea and experience freshness and the original taste of goodness.


Black tea also offers a variety of health benefits because it contains antioxidants and compounds that may help reduce inflammation in the body. Black tea contains a group of polyphenols that have antioxidant properties. Consuming antioxidants may help decrease the risk of chronic disease and improve your overall health. High blood pressure can cause many health complications. Drinking black tea on a regular basis may help decrease systolic and diastolic blood pressure. Black tea contains flavonoids, which are beneficial for heart health. Studies have found that regularly drinking black tea may help reduce the risk of heart disease.


Ceylon Green Tea

Grown and handpicked high in the central highlands of Sri Lanka, Ceylon Green Tea has a unique and pure taste that makes it the first choice for tea lovers. The traditional and sustainable methods we use guarantee natural goodness in every cup. Green tea is known to refresh your body and soul with the taste and goodness of pure Ceylon Green Tea, you can experience green tea at its finest. Created with traditional and sustainable methods, our green tea leaves are hand-picked to ensure they remain free from impurities.


Green tea is more than just a hydrating beverage. It’s loaded with antioxidants that have many health benefits, which may include improved brain function, fat loss, protecting against cancer and lowering the risk of heart disease. Some controlled studies show that green tea may cause mild reductions in blood sugar levels. It may also lower the risk of type 2 diabetes. Studies show that green tea may lead to increased weight loss. It may be particularly effective at reducing the dangerous abdominal fat


Ceylon White Tea

Ceylon Silver Tips, commonly known as White Tea, is a very rare Ceylon tea, entirely handmade and extremely limited in production just a few hundred kilos per year. White is the color of innocence, purity and goodness. White tea is made from the tender bud of a special variety of the tea plant, Camellia Sinensis, which is handpicked before the sun rises. Account of its rarity, it is a very exclusive tea that is available only in selected places. Yet it is an experience that must be savored at least once in a lifetime.


White tea is the least processed of the three teas. Because of this, it retains a high number of antioxidants. White tea is a great source of fluoride, catechins and tannins. Several studies have shown that these compounds can help fight bacteria that cause plaque on teeth. White tea and its compounds may protect the skin from damage linked to aging. This includes external damage from the sun’s UV rays and internal damage from cellular components that may harm the skin’s fiber network.

  3. Ceylon tea production exceeds 300,000 metric tons – 2000
